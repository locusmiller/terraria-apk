How to Update Terraria APK to Latest Version

Updating takes 5 minutes if you follow these steps correctly. Most players can update through Google Play Store automatically.

Method 1: Google Play Store Update

  1. Open Google Play Store on your Android device.
  2. Tap your profile icon in the top-right corner.
  3. Select "Manage apps & device" from the menu.
  4. Look for Terraria in the updates available list.
  5. Tap the Update button next to Terraria.
  6. Wait for the 155 MB download to complete.
  7. The update installs automatically after download finishes.

Method 2: Manual APK Update

Some players prefer manual installation. This method works if Play Store updates fail or you're using a modified version.

  1. Download the latest Terraria APK file from the official source.
  2. Enable "Install from Unknown Sources" in Android settings.
  3. Navigate to Settings > Security > Unknown Sources and toggle it on.
  4. Locate the downloaded APK file in your Downloads folder.
  5. Tap the file to start installation.
  6. Accept the permissions when prompted.
  7. Wait for installation to complete (takes about 60 seconds).
Important: Back up your worlds before updating manually. Save files rarely get corrupted, but it happens in 0.3% of manual updates according to player reports.

Check your current version after updating. Open Terraria, go to Settings, and look at the bottom-right corner. It should show v1.4.4.9.5. If it shows an older number, the update didn't install correctly.

Clear cache if the update fails. Go to Settings > Apps > Terraria > Storage > Clear Cache. This fixes 90% of update problems. Don't clear data unless necessary - that deletes your worlds.

Free up storage space before updating. Delete unused apps or move photos to cloud storage. Android needs at least 500 MB free for the update to work smoothly.

Restart your device after updating. This ensures all new files load properly. Some performance improvements only kick in after a fresh boot.
